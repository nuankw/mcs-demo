Task Overview

Your goal is to create True/False sentence pairs that are easily understood by people under common sense but can fool our AI agent, Carl, into judging them incorrectly. In this HIT, we ask you to write such sentence pairs for the following combination of ${domain} domain x ${scenario} scenario.

For example, consider the following True sentence:

${eg_true_1}, ${eg_true_2}. [True]

We can write its False complement:

${eg_false_1}, ${eg_false_2}. [False]

The two sentences together form a True/False sentence pair under ${domain} domain and ${scenario} scenario, challenging the common sense reasoning ability of our AI agent Carl.

Later, you will be redirected to the task page which contains all the detailed domain/scenario-specific instructions and more examples.
Now, let us first run you through the general information on the task payment and the overall task procedure.

Task Payment

In each task (this one included), you will be asked to create 3 sentence pairs.
For each pair, there are 3 possible payment outcomes depending on your creation quality:

  1. The base pay is $0.10 and will be automatically processed except for spamming cases.
  2. The basic bonus is $0.50 (total: $0.60/pair), and will be paid after validation if it meets one of the following conditions:
    • follows the instruction, and at least one of the two sentences in the pair fooled Carl
    • follows the instruction, and regardless of whether Carl is fooled, the pair is well-written, includes interesting common sense, and creative enough (subject to our internal validation decision)
  3. The full bonus is $0.90 (total: $1.00/pair) if it meets both conditions above.

Thus, in each task, you can earn at least ($0.10 + $0.50) * 3 = $1.80 and up to ($0.10 + $0.90) * 3 = $3.00 if spending quality time on creation. Please feel free to take as many tasks as available if you find them interesting & fairly paid.

Note: You can expect to receive all payments (base + bonus) in ~10 days.
Feel free to email us at mcspluslab@gmail.com with your worker ID if you think there is a delay or have any other concerns. We will reply ASAP. For non-urgent queries, please use the exit survey (explained below).

Overall Task Procedure

  1. After reading all the general instructions provided here, click the survey link at the bottom of this page, you will be redirected to the task page. Task-specific instructions and more examples are provided to better assist you with the creation task. Please follow all the requirements specified in our instructions.

     

  2. Enter sentence pair(s) and select the True/False (T/F) labels accordingly.

  3. To see if you successfully fooled our AI agent Carl, click on the "Test Carl with what I have!" button. The change of icons next to T/F labels (from gray question marks to green or red checkmarks) indicates whether Carl was fooled by the current sentence (hover over to see the text).

    Note that:

    • "Test Carl with what I have!" button will only be clickable if there's any input not tested. You can edit, re-test your inputs and play with Carl as many times as you like before the final submission.
    • "Inputs confirmed, submit!" button will only be clickable if all inputs are tested. And only one submission is allowed in each task, i.e. you cannot return to the editing page after the submission.
  4. Once fully tested and satisfied with your creation, click the "Inputs confirmed, submit!" button to submit your inputs (be cautious since you cannot undo submit!). A pop-up window will be displayed similar to the one below. Copy the completion code and enter it below on this page. Please fill out the exit survey each time you finish a task if you can. We appreciate your feedback!
  5. For any questions or assistance, kindly reach out to us via mcspluslab@gmail.com with your worker ID.
     

FAQs Collected from Exit Survey

​This part is optional reading, but please skim through it if you have time, as we think these are the common questions shared across MTurkers.

Q1. "I’m wondering for the comparison scenario, is it necessary to have both options in each of the sentences, or is it okay if the comparison comes from the two sentences as a pair?"
Ans:  To clarify, for the comparison scenario, both options need to be included in each of the sentences. Otherwise, we consider the pair belonging to the causal-and-effect scenario instead. 

Q2. "I'm also curious whether it is much more common to fool Carl with false statements than true ones, and whether it's more valuable to you one way or the other."
Ans:  We agree with your observation that among the data we collected, there are more false sentences which fooled Carl than the true sentences did. However, the fooling rates for both are around 50%. A trick we found to fool Carl in the true sentences is to do some negation, but you can totally use your own strategy. We don't have a strong preference, but would like you to equally try fooling Carl with True/False sentence.

Q3. "More examples on pairs that couldn't get bonus."
Ans:  Two major reasons for bonus reduction: ambiguity (hard to tell T/F) or being too specific (not common sense to all).​ We are adding more examples to the bonus reduction doc! (link in the creation instructions)

Q4. "Examples of pairs which received full bonus?"
Ans:  All the examples we give on the creation instructions page satisfy the well-written requirement. As to creativity, common instances & topics that are less seen in previous data will have an advantage (subject to internal validators' decision & yes we are aware of the subjectivity, so will be as generous as possible). Please refer to the topic list doc for inspiration. (link in the creation instructions)

Q5. "Can you share what fooled Carl exactly or mostly?"
Short answer: sorry, we don't know yet.
More study and research is required to give an acceptable answer, and we are only in the initial stage of this long journey (years to go). Thanks again for helping us out!
