Common Sense Reasoning HIT - Creation

Welcome! Please read this guideline carefully.

Task Overview

In this HIT, we ask you to test the common sense reasoning for our AI agent -- Carl, by developing sentence pairs for the combination of ${domain} domain and ${scenario} scenario.

Consider the following example:

${eg_true_1}, ${eg_true_2}. [True]

We can write its complement and change the correctness:

${eg_false_1}, ${eg_false_2}. [False]

Your goal is to write challenging pairs that are easily understood by people but are more likely to fool Carl into judging them incorrectly.

Payment

The base pay for completing this task is ${base_pay}, and the bonus pay is ${sentence_bonus} * your score. Your score is the number of sentences where you were able to successfully fool our agent Carl.
In our internal study, we usually find ourselves able to fool once in each pair, i.e. our score = 3.

Note: You can expect to receive the full payment (base + bonus) in ${how_many_days} days (we will validate and pay ASAP). Kindly ensure that all the sentences abide by our instructions, else your submission may not be eligible for a bonus, or could be completely rejected.

Task Details

  1. To begin this task, click the link below on this page.
    Note: Do not change the URL as doing so will interfere with a proper recording of your HIT.

     

  2. Read the instructions carefully on our website as they provide the definitions and examples needed to construct valid sentences.

  3. Enter sentence pairs strictly related to ${domain} and ${scenario} and select the True/False (T/F) labels accordingly.

  4. To see if you successfully fooled our AI agent Carl, click on the "Test Carl with what I have!" button. The result will show up next to the input fields (hover over the icon to see the result).
    Note that you can change and test your inputs as many times as you like.

  5. After you enter all 3 sentence pairs and have tested all your revisions, you can click the "Inputs confirmed, submit!" button to finish this HIT. A pop-up window will be displayed similar to the one below.
    Note that after you submit - you can no longer go back to edit your inputs!

  6. To complete your HIT, please copy and paste the complete code on MTurk. Please fill out the optional survey to help us improve the study. We appreciate your feedback!
  7. For any questions or assistance, kindly reach out to us via mcspluslab@gmail.com with your worker ID.
     

FAQs Collected from Exit Survey

​This part is optional reading, but please skim through it if you have time, as we think these are the common questions shared across MTurkers.

Q1. "I wasn't sure if I was supposed to fool him with both lines or just the false one? I think it was just the false line right? I couldn't find in the instructions where it was explicitly stated."
Ans: You can fool Carl in any sentence and as many as you can (each valid sentence that fooled Carl will get you ${sentence_bonus} in bonus. The green checkmark next to the sentence (hover to see what it means) indicates that you fooled Carl, and the red one indicates you didn't.

Q2. "Is it correct to say that the highest pay would be for submittable answers with six green checkmarks?"
Ans: Yes, it is correct (assuming your inputs follow all the rules).
With that being said, if a Carl-fooling sentence pair is well-constructed and creative enough, we tend to give a full bonus even if it is created for the wrong domain/scenario. However, please try your best to follow the domain/scenario as it will largely reduce our post-process work and thus speed up the bonus payment! Thank you.

Q3. "I want to know if the examples provided are all examples conforming to the specific domains you were asking for in this HIT, or if they were generalized examples."
Ans: Yes, all the examples provided are conforming to the specific domain and scenario asked in this HIT.

​Q4. "I was curious as to how much the sentences should be varied between the pair. Should they follow the template of the example, where the subjects are switched, and the sentence is essentially the same?  Or should they be constructed differently to make it harder for the AI?"
Ans: Yes, please follow the template to make sure that each sentence pair is contrastive (True vs. False) yet still about the same/very similar situation.

Q5. "I wasn't sure whether I should spell out each number or not. The examples were all spelled out and the instructions said to do that for numbers over 1000, but did not specify if you should ONLY do that for those higher numbers?"
Ans: For numbers less than 1000, you can use any format of your choice that reads naturally in a sentence. If you'd like to use any numbers outside of non-negative integers, we ask you to describe them in the most common-sense way. Lastly, please try to only include numeracy for the numerical-social, numerical-physical and time domains.

Q6. "Will you put in the directions on how many of these HITs a person can complete, if they can do more than 1 or just 1?"
Ans: Please do as many as you can. We (this common-sense reasoning HIT series) do not have any implicit constraint on the number of HITs each qualified MTurkers can do. However, please make sure that your creations are not repetitive. If you plan to do multiple HITs (and we will continue to publish more), we'd recommend you to keep a list of your creations somewhere to avoid repetition.

Q7. "It would be helpful to know if the AI is fooled by misunderstanding objects or context more often."
Ans: Nice catch! This is a very difficult question even for AI experts and researchers to have concrete answers for.
Indeed, one major reason that we started this project is to find out some clues to answer such a great question, or at least to promote discoveries and deeper understandings of common sense reasoning in language models (yes, our AI Agent Carl is equipped with a language model).
We are very interested to hear your thoughts on such questions & welcome all interesting ideas and discussions, please do consider sharing them with us either in the exit survey or at mcspluslab@gmail.com. All insightful ideas will be properly acknowledged!

Q8. "I need more help on how to fool him!"
Ans: Sure! We totally understand and sometimes also experience difficulties fooling Carl. We are composing a new document to share inspiration and fooling tricks. Stay tuned!

Q9. "I’m wondering for the comparison ones, is it necessary to have both options in each of the sentences, or is it okay if the comparison comes from the two sentences as a pair?"
Ans:  To clarify, for the comparison scenario, both options need to be included in each of the sentences. Otherwise, we consider the pair belonging to the causal-and-effect scenario instead.
