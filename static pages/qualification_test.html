First, we want to assess whether the given sentence relies on everyday, common sense understanding OR factual, specialized knowledge.

Common sense

  • Knowledge of common, everyday ideas that people learn just from experience.

    • e.g. It is easier to climb over three feet compared to a six feet fence.

    e.g. If one can earn more per month than they spend, then they can save money.

Specialized

  • Knowledge of subjects such as physics, biology, engineering, etc. -- i.e. topics that require formal education, and are thus likely to contain technical jargons.

    • e.g. One needs to manually configure the port number for providing remote access through the data link layer.

  • We also consider news, i.e. current or historical events as specialized.

    • e.g. People are hopeful towards expecting a vaccine for covid-19 by the end of the year.

    e.g. Carbon monoxide was used on a large scale during the Holocaust at some Nazi German extermination camps.

Lastly, we define that a desired sentence will have two features associated with it – Scenario AND Domain. We will illustrate them and their subtypes with examples below.

Scenarios

Refers to the type of reasoning that sentence requires in order to assess its correctness (True/False). There are three types of scenarios as follows:

  • Cause & Effect: It answers the “Why” question or predicts what is likely to happen next (effect), given an event that has occurred (cause).

    • e.g. Given the hour long queue at the entrance, we can't shop within minutes.

  • Comparison: It aims to compare two plausible reasons or concepts, for a given daily event.

    • e.g. If we spill over milk on the floor, it’s better to clean it with a mop instead of a broom.

  • Numeracy: It focuses on simple arithmetic skills (add, multiply, etc.) and concepts such as rate, percentage, probability, etc.

    • e.g. Given his $1000 monthly income and no money in savings, a rent of $1500 raises the risk of facing an eviction.

Domains

Refers to the type of knowledge, primarily categorized as social, physical and time domain. The three types are explained as follows:

  • Social: It focuses on people and social behavior, particularly attributes like personality, emotions and actions.

    • e.g. Sam being an orderly person, cannot withstand the litter in his kitchen.

  • Physical: Key aspects include the knowledge of daily objects, location, motion, etc.

    • e.g. If we spill over milk on the floor, it’s better to clean it with a mop instead of a broom.

  • Time: Knowledge regarding scheduling activities and their durations.

    • e.g. Given the hour long queue at the entrance, we can't shop within minutes.
